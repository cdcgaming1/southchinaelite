SOUTH CHINA ELITE

While Hong Kong’s once revered luxury goods

market has declined markedly in recent years, Wong

says that Lamborghini has been somewhat shielded from

its impact – a fact he attributes to the company’s unique

standing as a global icon of achievement.

“We tend to occupy the ultra-luxury market,” he

explains. “For our client base the luxury car is actually a

small part of their assets so it’s more on a sentiment basis.

“With a strong brand like Lamborghini, it’s viewed

as a benchmark for success so we have a strong following

in Hong Kong and we’re targeting a bigger audience than

emerging markets.

“In China for instance, because we have a dealership

there, most of our customers are entrepreneurs or

owners of companies but here we actually see a lot of

professionals becoming our customers, like lawyers,

doctors, bankers – these guys are making the kind of

dough to put them in that position.

“These are guys that have their life in their own

hands and they know what they are doing.”

Lamborghini is also seeing renewed interest from

female customers, who are increasingly attracted to the

supercar market as a symbol of their own power and

independence. Naturally, it’s a market Wong welcomes

with open arms.

儘管香港曾經如日中天的奢侈品市場近年顯著下滑，

但黃卓徽表示，林寶堅尼並沒有受其影響。他認為這是由

於品牌在全球均被視為成功標誌的獨特地位使然。

他解釋說︰「我們傾向於佔領超級奢侈市場。對我們

的客戶群而言，豪華座駕只佔他們資產的一小部分，因此

他們多是出於情愫而購買。

「林寶堅尼這樣的優質品牌被視為成功的標誌，因此

在香港有不少追隨者，並且相對於新興市場，我們有更大

的目標客戶群。

「比如中國內地，我們在那裡有經銷商，客戶大多

是企業家或公司老闆。但在香港，很多專業人士如律師、

醫生和銀行家，亦是我們的客戶。這些客戶擁有足夠的財

力，並對自己的生活駕馭自如，知道自己在做什麼。」

林寶堅尼也越來越受女性客戶追捧，她們逐漸被超跑

市場所吸引，視之為權力和獨立的象徵。自然，這也是黃

卓徽非常歡迎的市場。
