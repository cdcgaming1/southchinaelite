在經濟繁榮階段，不少企業憑藉獨特的產品及吸引的營

銷策略得以迅速發展。這樣的繁榮期可延續5年至10年。然

而，不少企業卻沒有為經濟週期的轉折點做好準備。他們在

高峰前過度擴張，故難以在衰退期從客戶處收取債務及向銀

行取得額外貸款。

最後，它們不得不出售業務，甚至破產。準確預測轉

折點看似很困難，但歷史數據就經濟週期的長短提供了不少

線索。企業應在對上一個低谷後4.3年或對上一個高峰後5.4

年，為逆週期策略做好準備。若企業在經濟衰退時擁有充裕

的資金，應在低谷階段購買資產並擴展業務——這一般在對

上一個高峰1.5年後出現。

Summary

總結

Many companies grow fast in economic booms with

unique products and attractive marketing strategies.

Their booming period may last for five to 10 years.

However, some of them don’t prepare sufficiently for

turning points in economics cycles. They over-expand

themselves before a peak and therefore find it hard

to collect debts from their customers and be provided

additional loans from banks in a recession period.

Finally, they sell off their business and even go

bankrupt. Predicting the exact turning points seems

difficult, however statistics from history provide clues on

interval estimates of economic cycles. A company should

prepare itself for countercyclical corporate strategies 4.3

years after the last trough or 5.4 years after the last peak. If

the company is cash-rich in economic downturns, it should

acquire assets and expand business at the trough which

tends to occur one to 1.5 years after the last peak.
