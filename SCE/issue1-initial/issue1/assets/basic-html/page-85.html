In general, the Fed rate and corresponding mortgage

rate hike remains one of the key swing factors of the

physical market. Hong Kong banks have been reducing

mortgage spread from HIBOR (HK Interbank Rate)

+170bps in 4Q15 to HIBOR +128bps in March 2017, to

offset the 50bps Fed rate hike since December 2016. It is

worth noting that room for a mortgage rate spread cut

has been reducing – both the speed and magnitude of

the spread cuts are declining.

Morgan Stanley’s US economists are assuming six

more rate hikes in the US by end-2018. If this were to

happen and if HIBOR follows a similar trend to the Fed

(due to the US peg), the local mortgage rate could double

from the current level and could impact not only new

buyers but also existing owners.

Despite brisk sales at Cullinan West and the Kai Tak

area this year, we estimate residential prices will drop

by 5% in 2017 driven by higher rates, increasing supply,

rising risk of cooling measures and competition for land

from Chinese developers, all driving margins lower.

These risks can be mitigated by higher global

reflation and economic growth. For example, a 5% annual

salary growth can offset 50bps of mortgage rate hikes

from an affordability ratio point of view.
