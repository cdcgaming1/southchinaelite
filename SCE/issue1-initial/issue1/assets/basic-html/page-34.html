SOUTH CHINA ELITE

Since competition for their funds is extremely

keen, all players nowadays have to change and develop

business strategies that fit their own strengths or unique

characteristics. For example, the mega banks like UBS

and JP Morgan have the ability to provide financial

services to the ultra-HNWIs (UHNWIs) who have assets

in excess of US$50 million, with some of them in the

hundreds of millions level. These UHNWIs usually have

their own professional financial advisors and demand

impeccable services which cannot be offered by the

smaller banks or IFAs. Their investment requirements

usually extend to investment banking services such

as overseas acquisitions, issuance of corporate bonds,

financial advisory services and international business

purchases such as commercial real estates. The big

global banks have all the supporting capabilities. The

fee-income generating potential is extensive.

The boutique or pure-play banks come mainly

from Switzerland and Liechtenstein. Their strengths

are inherent in the ability to provide personalized

services with unique investment opportunities in

Switzerland and Europe, in conjunction with family

trusts and foundations. They are usually strong in

discretionary portfolio management. Their market

segments encompass HNWIs who demand exclusivity.

In Asia, the two locomotives of the growth of

individual wealth are China and India. Overall, the

growth in Asia has already surpassed Europe and

is catching up with North America. Singapore has

the advantage for Indian sub-continent clients, viz

India, Pakistan, Bangladesh and Sri Lanka. Hong

Kong dominates over the Greater China region, which

includes mainland China, Hong Kong and Taiwan.

According to Frank Knight’s 2016 Wealth Report,

there are now more than 13 million millionaires across

the globe, up from 8.7 million in 2005. These HNWIs

own from one million to billions of dollars of assets,

amounting to US$66 trillion, which is more than the

value of all global equities. The US is still the leader,

with 1.25 million millionaires, followed by 490,500 in

China, 253,500 in the UK and 247,800 in India.
