SOUTH CHINA ELITE

BB: As you said earlier, you spend your life

traveling the world. You must be accustomed to

traveling these days?

BT:

Yes and I have several homes but I spend very

little time in my own home. For the past 15 years I

wouldn’t have spent any more than two weeks a year

in my own home. Those of us who travel the world as

much as I do tend to think of home as being wherever

we are. We think on a global scale.

But I can’t complain. I fly everyweek to somewhere,

it’s how I get to work and how I get to wherever I need

to be for whatever reason. Essentially my work takes

me to many countries and many situations that are

both friendly and sometimes not so.

BB: More and more companies these days are

looking to be global companies. How beneficial is it

that you yourself operate globally and understand

the nature of true globalization?

BT:

Well that’s correct in as much as I operate in

a number of different countries and have clients all

around the world. But it’s a privilege really to be able

to work with boards, CEOs and Managing Directors

that are keen to see growth for their company not

just regionally but globally. To assist in formulating

innovative strategies and to assist them in the

execution of those strategies using my experience is

very rewarding when you see it come to fruition.

BB: How else are you involved in organizations?

BT:

I can honesty state that everything I do I love

doing,fromboardadvisoryanddevelopmenttoteaching

seminars to raising money for various charities – from

poverty to women’s causes and building classrooms

in rural communities. In every project, program or

initiative I have had the opportunity to learn valuable

lessons about people, places and problems.

BB: Can you provide some insight into how you

practically go about changing a company’s direction

or adjusting its culture?

BT:

Traditionally speaking – and it’s the same

today – most companies go into what they call an

annual planning or strategy session which culminates

in beautiful documentation with a beautiful collection

of charts and budgets. But the reality when you go into

the research is that over 90% of companies fail to meet

their objectives annually and predominantly that is

due to over-estimation of the market, under-estimation

or under-appreciation of the culture they operate in.

In some cases it’s just a rehash of the budget and

strategy documents of previous years with very minor

consideration for inflation, and also the residency

of corporate pride and management ego. This is why

many fail.
