SOUTH CHINA ELITE

What about the ability to innovate in

China?

In fairness, innovation efforts in China in recent years

have been breath taking with the growth in innovation

labs and the encouragement by the Central Government

for business enterprise to search for innovative solutions.

It is clearly a cornerstone of the economy for business

growth and development. What might help and accelerate

this key functioning is the constant coaching of this process

with profound understanding of the necessary culture

in order to support innovation within any organization.

For example, in Israel you see that companies are

constantly coming up with novel products. It’s a 24-hour,

360-degree challenge and opportunity for the growing

economy. And whether it is about developing security

systems in Israel or games in Taiwan or new technologies

in the San Francisco Bay Area, we must be reminded that

the choice is crystal clear: innovate or die.

Simply stated, the challenge for China is its ability

to innovate in this next decade as it is becoming more

predictable as more and more Chinese Brands become

household names.

The thinking in Israel is entirely different. Their

starting point is, “We are surrounded. We’ve got to

innovate to survive.” Clearly it’s about courage, risk

and taking initiative. You only have to look at how

many Israeli companies are now listed on the NASDAQ

exchange. But it all starts with leadership, the way

management and decision-making takes place and

the time it takes to get things done coupled with the

additional time to market. The Israeli culture supports

innovation, competitiveness and internal collaboration.

Perhaps there is a lesson here for all of us to learn from?
