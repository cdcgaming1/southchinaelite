SOUTH CHINA ELITE

For over 50 years of academic programs in all

classes of society – particularly in Hong Kong and

Singapore, among others – medicine, engineering

and professional disciplines like law and business

have been the most attractive programs for parents to

encourage their children to enrol in. However, while

all of these programs have their merits and benefits,

it is my faithful reminder that without the study of

human interaction, behaviour and communications,

the students would find themselves irrelevant in a fast-

changing world and would be restricted in enabling

their professional effectiveness in a given situation.

It is clearly evidenced when new graduates are

undergoing job interviews; many are unable to piece

together a complete and comprehensible sentence. In

fact, a considerable number of graduates utterly lack

social skills and even a basic understanding of human

behaviour. The evident uneasiness in interviews

and group settings of even those armed with five

years of work experience is even more troubling in

management education.

What are the caveats to further

management education?

Hypercompetitive competition and economic

turbulence never before seen at this rate have caused

all of us to be focused around growth and change in the

midst of increasing shrinking profitability. In this era of

rapid innovation, we are charged with managing value

whilst being reshaped by globalization and technology,

which will reshape our future.

As we continue to devote ourselves to serving

the global community through education in both

management andbusiness programs throughDBA, PhD,

EMBA and MBA and other offerings, we must recognize

that our study together is really a commitment and

conviction that good leadership and management can

indeed make countries, companies and societies more

capable and more vibrant.

Whilst a growth in enrolment in management

educationhasbeennoticedinrecentyears,onecuriously

observes that motivation for candidacy varies. Some,

for example, would treasure the opportunity to have

advanced one’s standing in a business career, while

others are looking to fill space on an office wall or a

conversation piece on the golf course. Despite varying

motivation, it’s fair to acknowledge that the opportunity

is really about advancing one’s “intellectual ambition”

and advancing our organization’s inclusion in the

global marketplace.

Our role as business educators is to continue our

collaboration with our community and society, inspired

by innovation and authentic leadership as we go forth

to engage the exciting universe of commerce.
