在亞洲，銀行依然是管理多數高淨值人士資產的機構。

Critical Success Factors

There are various types of financial institutions

handling the wealth of the HNWIs (High Net Worth

Individuals). In Hong Kong and Singapore, these consist

mainly of the banks, securities houses, independent

financial advisors (IFAs), asset management and fund

companies, family offices and some private equity

firms. All these companies are regulated by either MAS

(Monetary Authority of Singapore), HKMA (Hong Kong

Monetary Authority) or SFC (Securities and Futures

Commission) in Hong Kong.

In Asia, the banks still hold the largest amount of

AUM of the HNWIs. UBS, for example, had about US$274

billion of AUM in 2016; followed by Citibank and Credit

Suisse (globally, UBS maintains the biggest AUM of

about US$1 trillion). Total AUM of the largest 20 banks

amounted to about US$1.47 trillion in Asia. The other

non-bank financial institutions retain inconsequential

assets. Asset and fund management companies hold

investment funds mainly from banks (invested by

HNWIs), domestic and global institutional investors.

IFAs and family offices hold other scattered assets.

The critical determinant factors for a successful,

profitable and sustainable private banking and wealth

management business are the financial institution’s

ability to attract and maintain the wealth of the HNWIs,

at an optimal cost-to-income ratio.
