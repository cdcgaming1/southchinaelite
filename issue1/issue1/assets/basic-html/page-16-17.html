SOUTH CHINA ELITE

BB: Is that why you continue to study to this

day?

BT:

Yes because the more you know, or think you

know, the more you realize that you don’t actually

know much at all. It’s this whole notion of enquiry.

When you’re teaching Nobel Prize winners and

national scholars and the best brains in the world at

the best schools, that’s a pretty good way to realize

how much more there is to know. And that’s not false

modesty, it’s true – I still go to some classes and have no

clue what they are talking about!

So that is reinforcement of what you don’t know.

When you have a modest amount of diverse clients who

are paying you good money, you owe it to them to not

only be able to keep up but to be a valuable contributor

to your clients.

Think about it in the context of medicine. Do you

really want your brain operation from someone that

earned their degree many, many years ago and has

done a couple of online courses since or do you want

to know that your surgeon is really employing state of

the art technology? Of course you want the very best!

If you have a patient on the table and you must

execute a corporate operation, who do you really want

to operate on you? The only way is to not only study

regularly and with a passion but also to practice the

very latest theories and methods.

本思齊：這是你至今一直學習的原因嗎？

Brian To博士：是的。當一個人知道得愈多，或認為自己

知道得愈多時，才發覺自己知道的其實很少。這就是研究探索

的核心。

在著名學府教導諾貝爾獎得主、各國頂級學者及全球精

英時，讓人明白到自己要學習的還有很多！這不是客氣話，

而是真確的——有時候，我出席某些課程時，還真的不知道

他們在談甚麼！

這有助於找出知識的盲點。當你有一定數量、來自不

同背景的客戶向你支付學費時，你的責任就不限於與他們接

軌，而更需要為他們提供寶貴意見。

以醫學為例，如果要接受腦部手術，你會選擇一位畢業

多年，後來完成了幾個遙距課程的醫生，還是一個真正採用

最先進技術的醫生？選擇最好的，是人之常情。

如果躺在手術床上的病人是一家企業，你希望誰來操

刀？唯一的選擇，就是專心致志地學習，並應用最新的理論

及方法。
